# Design Principles — Gee & Geesus

## 1. Dark craft, not dark corporate

Every surface uses the anthracite palette — never pure black (#000), never generic dark gray. The dark theme should feel like warm wood and leather in low light, not a tech dashboard. Use the lightest anthracite (#4a4a4a) for subtle hover states and dividers, not for large surfaces.

**Correct:** Card background #2e2e2e with #3a3a3a border, text #d4d4d4
**Incorrect:** Pure black background, bright white cards, blue links

## 2. One action per view, everything else recedes

Every screen has exactly one primary CTA — always "Zarezervovat" in some form. Secondary actions (social links, navigation, read more) use ghost or text styles. If two buttons compete for attention, one of them is wrong.

**Correct:** Hero has one white CTA button; nav booking button is smaller and outlined
**Incorrect:** Hero with "Zarezervovat" AND "Zjistit více" AND "Instagram" all as filled buttons

## 3. Photos do the selling, text does the closing

The barbershop's real photos are the strongest asset. Use them large, use them often, let them breathe. Text should be short, punchy, and in service of the image — not competing with it. Body copy stays under 3 sentences per block. Headlines are 5 words or fewer.

**Correct:** Full-bleed owner photo with a two-line quote overlaid
**Incorrect:** A paragraph of text with a small thumbnail beside it

## 4. Left-align the story, center the ritual

Body text and about-style sections are left-aligned — people read left to right, don't fight it. Only the "ritual" moments get centered: hero, booking CTA, section headers. This creates visual rhythm between storytelling (left) and action (center).

**Correct:** About section has left-aligned paragraphs beside a photo. Services section has centered header, left-aligned card content.
**Incorrect:** Every section is centered. Every paragraph is centered.

## 5. Respect the silence

White space (dark space, in our case) is not wasted space. Sections need room to breathe — minimum 96px vertical padding on desktop, 64px on mobile. Don't fill every gap with decorative elements. The anthracite background IS the design element.

**Correct:** Gallery grid with 12px gaps, surrounded by 96px padding above and below
**Incorrect:** Gallery crammed between sections with 24px margins
